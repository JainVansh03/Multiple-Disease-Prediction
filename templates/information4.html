What steps can be taken if a person has a kidney disease?

Manage underlying conditions:

Managing underlying conditions, such as diabetes or high blood pressure, is an important part of treating kidney failure. Treating and managing these conditions may help improve kidney function and prevent further damage to the kidneys. This may involve medications, lifestyle changes, or a combination of both. It's important to work closely with your healthcare provider to develop an individualized treatment plan for managing underlying conditions and preventing kidney failure.

Medications:

Medications may be prescribed to manage symptoms of kidney failure and prevent further damage to the kidneys. Depending on the underlying cause of kidney failure, different types of medications may be used, such as diuretics to help remove excess fluid from the body or medications to control blood pressure

Dialysis:

Dialysis is a medical procedure that helps remove waste and excess fluids from the blood when the kidneys can no longer perform this function. There are two main types of dialysis: hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis. Hemodialysis involves removing blood from the body, filtering it through a machine, and then returning it to the body. Peritoneal dialysis involves using the lining of the abdominal cavity to filter waste and excess fluids from the blood.

Kidney transplant:

A kidney transplant is a surgical procedure that involves replacing a diseased or damaged kidney with a healthy kidney from a donor. Kidney transplants can be a highly effective treatment for kidney failure, offering a better quality of life and longer survival than dialysis. However, not everyone is a candidate for a kidney transplant, and the procedure can come with risks and complications, including rejection of the new kidney by the recipient's immune system.

Lifestyle changes:

Making lifestyle changes can help manage symptoms of kidney failure and improve overall health. This may include quitting smoking, maintaining a healthy weight, eating a balanced and low-sodium diet, staying physically active, and managing stress. These lifestyle changes can help prevent or manage underlying conditions that contribute to kidney failure
